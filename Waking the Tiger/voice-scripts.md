# Voice Scripts for "Waking the Tiger" Cards

These scripts are designed for a gentle, grounding voice. Each one walks you through the concept or practice in a way that feels supportive and clear.

---

## CORE CONCEPTS

### Script 1: Somatic Experiencing

Let's explore Somatic Experiencing together.

This is a body-centered approach to healing trauma. Rather than seeing trauma as something wrong with your mind, we understand it as an incomplete response in your body - something that wants to finish.

Think of it this way: trauma isn't primarily psychological. It's a physiological response that got interrupted and never completed.

Somatic Experiencing works with what we call your "felt sense" - your body's internal awareness. Instead of reliving traumatic memories, we work with your instinctual responses, gently helping your body complete what it started.

This is a natural process. Your body already knows how to heal. We're just creating space for that to happen.

Take a moment now to simply notice sensations in your body, without trying to change them. That's where we begin.

---

### Script 2: The Felt Sense

Let's talk about the felt sense.

This is your body's way of knowing - not through thoughts, but through sensation.

It's different from emotions. The felt sense is the raw, physical experience underneath feelings. It's how you experience yourself as a living organism.

Right now, notice how you know you're comfortable or uncomfortable. What tells you that? Not your thoughts - the actual sensations in your body.

Maybe there's warmth or coolness. Tightness or openness. Heaviness or lightness. These sensations are your felt sense speaking.

This awareness is essential for healing. It's the medium through which your body communicates what it needs.

So here's a simple practice: Ask yourself, "How do I know I feel this way right now?" Then notice what sensations give you that information.

That's your felt sense. Learn to trust it.

---

### Script 3: The Immobility Response

Let's understand the freeze response.

When you face a threat, your body has three options: fight, flight, or freeze.

The freeze response happens when you perceive the threat as inescapable. Your body becomes very still, very quiet. It's an ancient survival mechanism.

In nature, animals freeze and then, once safe, they shake and tremble to discharge that frozen energy. They complete the response and move on.

But as humans, we often override this natural discharge. We suppress the shaking. We hold ourselves still. And that energy stays bound in our nervous system.

This is the core mechanism of trauma - not the event itself, but this trapped, undischarged energy.

Your symptoms aren't signs that something's wrong with you. They're your body's way of holding this energy until it's safe to release it.

Notice now: do you ever feel stuck or frozen? That's your body's protective response. And it can be gently, safely released.

---

### Script 4: Energy Model of Trauma

Let's explore trauma as trapped energy.

Imagine a car with the accelerator pressed all the way down and the brakes fully engaged at the same time. The engine is roaring, but the car can't move.

That's what trauma feels like in your nervous system. There's tremendous energy - fight or flight energy - but it's frozen in place.

This energy isn't bad. It needs to be there for survival. But it needs to discharge for you to feel whole again.

Animals do this naturally. They tremble, they shake, they move. And then they're fine.

Your symptoms - the anxiety, the tension, the feeling of being on edge - these aren't problems to fix. They're your body's way of containing this energy until it can safely flow through and out.

Healing isn't about getting rid of something. It's about allowing what's already there to complete its natural cycle.

Think of it as releasing stored energy, not fixing what's broken. Because you're not broken. You're holding power that wants to move.

## HEALING PRINCIPLES

### Script 5: The Thawing Process

Let's talk about thawing.

When something is frozen, you don't force it to melt. You let it warm gradually, naturally, at its own pace.

Healing trauma works the same way. We're gently completing responses that got interrupted - allowing your body to finish what it started.

A key part of this is uncoupling immobility from fear. Right now, being still might feel dangerous. We'll help your body learn that stillness can be safe again.

We're also restoring your access to natural survival responses - the ability to move, to orient, to act.

There's a saying: "Acts must be carried through to their completion." Your body wants to complete these responses. We're just creating the conditions for that to happen.

This happens at your body's own pace. Never forced. Never rushed. Just gentle, patient thawing.

Allow small movements and sensations to complete naturally. Trust the process. Your body knows what to do.

---

### Script 6: Resources and Resilience

Let's build your resources.

Healing isn't just about processing trauma. It's about strengthening what supports you - your resources.

Resources can be internal or external. A memory of safety. A place that feels good. A person who cares about you. Your own strength. The ground beneath your feet.

Building resources means building your confidence that you can respond to threats. That you have capacity. That you're not helpless.

One powerful resource is grounding - feeling your connection to your body and the earth. Feel your feet now. Notice the support beneath you.

We're also developing what's called "springiness" - the capacity to yield and recover. Not rigid strength, but flexible resilience.

Notice right now: what makes you feel supported? Safe? Strong? Even a little bit?

Build your awareness of these resources. They're your foundation for all the healing work you'll do.

---

### Script 7: Renegotiation

Let's explore renegotiation.

This is about completing unfinished responses in present time - not by reliving the past, but by working with sensation now.

We gradually build arousal - that activated energy - while increasing your resources at the same time. So you feel more capable as intensity increases.

A key part of this is separating excitement from fear. Right now, activation might automatically mean danger. But we can uncouple these.

We're helping you move from helplessness to agency. From frozen to free.

This doesn't happen through talking about what happened. It happens through sensation. Through noticing impulses to move and gently exploring completing those movements.

Maybe there's an impulse to push, to turn, to run. We explore these slowly, safely, with full awareness.

This is renegotiation - rewriting the response at the level of your nervous system.

Notice now: is there any impulse to move? Any direction your body wants to go? Just notice. Don't force anything. This is the beginning.

---

### Script 8: What Animals Teach Us

Let's learn from animals.

Watch an animal that's been startled. After the threat passes, it shakes. It trembles. Energy moves through its body in waves. And then it goes back to grazing, to resting, to living.

Animals complete their survival responses fully. They discharge the energy that got activated. And they don't develop chronic trauma symptoms.

Humans are different. We override our instincts with our thoughts. We tell ourselves to hold still, to be calm, to not shake. And that energy stays trapped.

Reconnecting to your animal nature - your instinctual wisdom - is key to healing.

Your body already knows how to do this. The trembling, the shaking, the discharge - these aren't problems. They're solutions.

When you're in a safe place and you feel tremors or the urge to shake, allow it. Don't force it, but don't stop it either.

Let your body teach you. Let your animal nature guide the way. Trust this ancient wisdom. It's older and wiser than your thinking mind.

## SYMPTOMS TO RECOGNIZE

### Script 9: Hyperarousal

Let's recognize hyperarousal.

This is when your nervous system gets stuck in the "on" position. You can't rest. You can't relax. You're constantly scanning for danger.

Maybe you're jumpy, easily startled. Maybe sleep is difficult. Maybe you feel wound up all the time.

This isn't your fault. It's undischarged fight-or-flight energy. Your system is trying to protect you, but it's protecting you from threats that aren't here anymore.

Notice now: do you feel activated? Is your heart beating fast? Are your muscles tense? Are you scanning the room?

This is hyperarousal. And the good news is, this energy can be gently brought down.

Grounding helps. Feeling your feet on the floor. Noticing the support beneath you. Taking slower breaths.

You're safe right now. Your body just doesn't know it yet. We'll help it learn.

When you notice hyperarousal, don't fight it. Just acknowledge it. "This is my nervous system trying to protect me." And then gently use grounding to come back down.

---

### Script 10: Constriction

Let's explore constriction.

This is when your awareness narrows. Your perception gets tight. Your range of responses becomes limited.

It's like tunnel vision - you can only see what's right in front of you. You can't take in new information easily. Your behaviors become rigid, automatic.

This narrowing is protective. It helped you survive overwhelming situations. But when it becomes habitual, it limits your life.

Notice now: where do you feel tight or restricted? Is your breathing shallow? Is your focus narrow?

This is constriction. And we can gently expand it.

One practice is orienting - looking around, noticing your environment. Not searching for danger, but just seeing what's here.

Turn your head slowly. Notice colors, shapes, textures. Let your awareness expand outward.

Feel your peripheral vision opening. Feel your breathing deepen. Feel the tightness softening.

This is the antidote to constriction - gentle, gradual expansion of awareness.

---

### Script 11: Dissociation

Let's understand dissociation.

This is feeling disconnected from your body. Numbness. A sense of unreality. Like you're watching yourself from outside.

Maybe you feel spaced out. Maybe parts of your body feel like they're not there. Maybe you feel unreal, like you're in a dream.

This is a protective disconnection. When experience was too overwhelming, your consciousness split off from sensation. It's a brilliant survival strategy.

But it means you've lost connection with yourself. And that makes it hard to feel alive, to feel present, to feel real.

Notice now: can you feel your hands? Your feet? Do you feel present in your body, or does it feel far away?

If you feel disconnected, we'll work very gently to help you come back.

The pulsing shower exercise is one way - using water to help you feel each part of your body again, welcoming it back.

Start small. One hand. One foot. Gentle reconnection. Over time, you can inhabit your whole self again.

You're here. You're real. Your body is waiting to welcome you home.

---

### Script 12: Helplessness

Let's recognize helplessness.

This is the feeling of being trapped. Powerless. Unable to act. Like you have no choices, no agency, no control.

Maybe you struggle to make decisions. Maybe you feel like a victim of circumstances. Maybe taking action feels impossible.

This comes from incomplete defensive responses. At some point, your fight and flight were blocked. You couldn't escape. You couldn't fight back. And that froze into a sense of permanent helplessness.

But it's not permanent. This is a response, not who you are.

Notice now: is there something you want to do but feel you can't? Some small action that feels blocked?

We'll start tiny. The smallest movements. The smallest choices. Gradually building your capacity.

Maybe just shifting your weight. Turning your head. Choosing where to look.

These small acts restore agency. They teach your nervous system: "I can act. I have choice. I'm not helpless."

Over time, this capacity grows. From small movements to bigger ones. From stuck to free.

You have more power than you know. We'll help you find it.

## EXERCISES

### Script 13: Pulsing Shower Exercise

Let's practice the pulsing shower exercise.

You'll need a gentle pulsing showerhead and about ten minutes.

Set the water to a comfortable temperature - cool or warm, whatever feels good to you.

Start with your hands. Let the pulsing water touch one hand, then the other. As the water touches each part, say out loud or silently: "This is my hand. I welcome you back."

Move slowly up your arms. "This is my arm. I welcome you back."

Your face. Your neck. Your chest and back. Your legs. Your pelvis. Your feet.

Take your time with each area. Really feel the sensation of the water. Really welcome each part of your body back into your awareness.

If you don't have a pulsing shower, you can gently slap or tap different body parts briskly. Same intention - reconnecting, welcoming.

Do this daily if you can. It counteracts numbness and disconnection.

You're not broken. You're here. Each part of you is real and welcome.

This is your body. Welcome it home.

### Script 14: Picture Response Exercise

Let's practice tracking your body's responses.

You'll need some magazine pictures or photos - images of different things, people, places.

Before you look at the first picture, take a moment to sense your body. Feel your arms. Your legs. Where you're making contact with your chair or the floor. Notice your overall physical state.

Now look at the first picture. Notice immediately: do you like it, dislike it, or feel neutral?

Good. Now here's the key question: How do you know that's your response?

What sensations in your body tell you that you like, dislike, or feel neutral?

Maybe there's warmth or coolness. Expansion or contraction. A sense of moving toward or pulling away. Tension or ease.

Notice where in your body you feel this. Notice the quality - is it tense, fuzzy, jagged, smooth, heavy, light?

Stay with the sensations. Watch if they change.

Then move to the next picture at your own pace.

This practice teaches you to track your felt sense - the body's response underneath your reactions. This awareness is the foundation for all healing work.

---

### Script 15: Photo/Memory Exercise

Let's work with personal photos.

Find some photos from meaningful times in your life - childhood, important relationships, significant moments.

Before you look at the first photo, check in with your body. Feel your arms, your legs, where you're making contact with support.

Now look at the photo. Notice the emotional response that comes up.

Here's the key question: How do you know you feel this way?

Find the sensations underneath the emotion. Where do you feel it in your body? What's the quality?

Is there tension? Where? What does it feel like - tight, heavy, sharp, dull?

Is there energy? Does it move? In what direction?

How's your breathing? Your heartbeat? The feeling in your skin?

What material does this sensation feel like? Stone, water, wood, metal, air?

If the intensity gets too high, shift to a pleasant memory instead. We're building capacity, not overwhelming you.

This practice helps you understand emotion through bodily sensation. It helps you integrate your personal history through felt experience.

Take your time. Be gentle with yourself.

### Script 16: Grounding Exercise

Let's practice grounding.

Find an object that feels significant or comforting to you. Maybe a smooth stone, a soft fabric, something with texture and weight.

Hold it. Feel its texture. Its temperature. Its weight in your hand.

Now bring your awareness to your legs. Can you feel them? Feel down to your feet. Feel where your feet make contact with the floor.

Notice the pressure. The support. The solidity.

Imagine, if you like, roots growing from the soles of your feet down into the earth. Deep, strong roots.

Feel the ground supporting you. Holding you up. You don't have to hold yourself - the earth does that.

Feel your weight. Feel gravity. Feel the solid, reliable support beneath you.

This is grounding. This is the resource of stability.

Use this whenever you feel scattered, anxious, or disconnected. Feel your feet. Feel the earth. Feel supported.

You are held. You are safe. You are grounded.

### Script 17: Orienting Response

Let's practice the orienting response.

This is your natural ability to scan your environment and assess safety.

Imagine for a moment that you see sudden movement in your peripheral vision. What happens?

You freeze slightly. Your muscles tense. You become alert.

Your head turns toward the movement. Your eyes widen. Your muscles extend and lengthen as you look.

There's a quality of curiosity: "What is it?"

When you realize there's no threat, you return to a relaxed state. Your body knows it's safe.

This is the orienting response. It's natural, healthy, protective.

Right now, practice it gently. Turn your head and look around your space. Not searching for danger, just noticing what's here.

See the colors. The shapes. The light.

Notice: when you see there's no threat, can you relax? Can you soften?

This practice reconnects you with your natural capacity to assess your environment and return to ease.

You have this ability. Trust it.

### Script 18: Tremoring Exercise

Let's explore gentle tremoring.

First, make sure you're in a safe environment where you won't be disturbed.

This isn't about forcing anything. If trembling or shaking happens naturally during other practices, this is what's occurring.

If you feel the impulse to shake or tremor, allow it. Let it be gentle. Let it move at your body's pace.

Don't amplify it. Don't make it bigger. Just allow what wants to happen.

You might feel it in your legs first. Or your hands. Or your whole body might have small waves of movement.

As this happens, observe the sensations. Maybe there's warmth. Tingling. Moisture. A sense of energy moving.

This is how animals naturally discharge trauma. This is your body's ancient wisdom completing what it needs to complete.

Trust this process. It's not scary. It's natural.

If nothing happens, that's fine too. Don't force it. Just know that if tremoring does occur, it's your body healing itself.

Let it move through. Let it complete. This is your animal nature restoring balance.

### Script 19: Movement Completion

Let's practice completing interrupted movements.

Think gently about a response that got frozen - maybe the impulse to run, to push away, to turn toward or away from something.

We're going to explore completing that movement, but very slowly, and only while you feel safe and resourced.

First, ground yourself. Feel your feet. Feel supported.

Now, begin the movement at about one-tenth the speed you'd normally move. Extremely slowly.

Maybe you're slowly standing when you wanted to stand. Slowly pushing when you wanted to push. Slowly turning your head when you wanted to look away.

As you move, notice sensations. Stay with awareness of your body.

If it feels like too much, stop. Ground again. You're in charge.

The point isn't to relive anything. It's to complete the motor response your body wanted to make but couldn't.

For example, if you froze when you wanted to run, you might slowly, gently move your legs as if walking, while seated.

Notice the sensations as the movement completes. There might be relief. Warmth. A sense of something finishing.

This is renegotiation at the level of your nervous system. Gentle. Slow. Safe.

## ADDITIONAL CORE INSIGHTS

### Script 20: The Medusa Principle

Let's understand the Medusa principle.

In Greek mythology, if you looked directly at Medusa, you'd turn to stone - you'd freeze.

But Perseus defeated her by looking at her reflection in his shield, not directly at her.

This is how we work with trauma. We don't confront it directly - that will freeze you, just like Medusa's gaze.

Instead, we work with the reflection. We work with sensation in your body - the felt sense. We work sideways, gently, indirectly.

The direct memory, the traumatic event itself - that's Medusa. Looking right at it will freeze you.

But the sensations in your body right now - that's the reflection. That's your shield. That's how you approach safely.

Perseus also had resources: the winged horse, the golden sword. Your resources are your grounding, your resilience, your capacity.

So we approach trauma sideways, through sensation, with resources in place.

Not head-on through memory. Gently, through the body's wisdom.

This is how healing happens safely. With reflection, not confrontation.

### Script 21: Grounding (Principle)

Let's explore grounding as a core principle.

Grounding is your connection to body and earth. It's rooted stability.

It's the physical sense of being supported by the ground. Feeling your weight. Feeling solidity. Feeling presence.

Without grounding, all other healing work is shaky. Grounding is the essential foundation.

It's the antidote to feeling scattered, unreal, or disconnected.

Right now, feel your feet on the floor. Really feel them. Notice the support beneath you. Let yourself be held.

Feel your breath. Feel your weight. Feel gravity doing its work.

You don't have to hold yourself up. The earth does that for you.

This is grounding. Simple, essential, always available.

Whenever you feel lost, scared, or overwhelmed, come back to this. Feel your feet. Feel the earth. Feel supported.

This is your foundation. Everything builds from here.

### Script 22: Springiness

Let's explore springiness - dynamic resilience.

This isn't rigid strength. It's not about being hard or tough or unbending.

Springiness is the capacity to yield and recover. To bend without breaking. To absorb impact and bounce back.

Think of a tree in the wind. It doesn't stand rigid - it would snap. It bends, it sways, and then it returns.

This is resilience. This is aliveness. This is springiness.

When challenges come, can you yield? Can you flex? Can you return to center?

Or do you either stay rigid and risk breaking, or collapse and stay down?

Springiness is that quality of being responsive, alive, able to move with what comes.

Notice how you respond to the small challenges in your day. Do you tense and hold? Do you collapse? Or can you yield and recover?

This quality can be developed. Through practice. Through awareness.

Springiness is the sign of a nervous system that's healing - flexible, responsive, alive.

Cultivate this quality. It's the opposite of trauma's rigidity and collapse.

### Script 23: Uncoupling

Let's talk about uncoupling.

Right now, if you're living with trauma, arousal probably means danger. When your energy rises, it automatically couples with fear or panic.

Your heart beats faster and your mind screams, "Danger!"

But arousal itself isn't dangerous. Excitement, aliveness, passion - these are forms of arousal too.

Healing means uncoupling - separating arousal from fear. Separating excitement from anxiety.

So that when energy rises in your body, it doesn't automatically mean something's wrong.

You can feel activated and know you're safe. You can feel excitement and not panic.

This allows joy back into your life. It allows aliveness. It allows you to feel without being overwhelmed.

This uncoupling happens gradually, through the work of renegotiation. As you build resources and complete responses, your nervous system learns:

"Arousal doesn't equal danger. I can feel energy and be safe."

Notice when activation arises in you. Pause. Ask: Is this necessarily dangerous? Or could some of this energy be excitement? Curiosity? Life?

Over time, you reclaim the full spectrum of feeling. Energy becomes safe again.

### Script 24: The Felt Sense Practice

Let's end with a simple felt sense practice.

Find a comfortable position. Let yourself be supported by whatever you're sitting or lying on.

Notice the sensations of contact. Where your body touches the surface beneath you. Where your clothing touches your skin.

Now bring your awareness underneath your skin. What do you notice inside your body?

Maybe there's warmth or coolness. Tightness or spaciousness. Movement or stillness.

You don't need to change anything. Just notice.

Ask yourself: "How do I know I feel comfortable right now?" Or if you don't feel comfortable, "How do I know I feel uncomfortable?"

What specific sensations give you this information?

Notice how, as you pay attention, the sensations might shift or change. Your awareness itself affects your experience.

This is the felt sense - your body's way of knowing. Your direct experience of being alive.

This is where healing happens. Not in your thoughts, but here, in the living sensations of your body.

Take a few more moments just to be with this. To feel. To sense. To be.

This is enough. You're doing it. This is the practice.
